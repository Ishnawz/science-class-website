<h1>Grade 3 Science Class</h1>
<h2>Understanding the Water Cycle</h2>
<p><strong>Objective:</strong> Students will understand the water cycle, including evaporation, condensation, precipitation, and collection, through interactive activities and observations.</p>
<h3>Materials Needed:</h3>
<ul>
<li>Water</li>
<li>Clear plastic cups</li>
<li>Markers</li>
<li>Small mirrors</li>
<li>Cotton balls</li>
<li>Ice cubes</li>
<li>Sunlight or a lamp</li>
</ul>
<h3>Lesson Structure:</h3>
<ol>
<li><strong>Introduction:</strong> Discuss where rain comes from and key water cycle terms.</li>
<li><strong>Evaporation Experiment:</strong> Observe water levels in the sun and shade.</li>
<li><strong>Condensation Demonstration:</strong> Use mirrors to observe condensation.</li>
<li><strong>Precipitation Simulation:</strong> Use cotton balls soaked in water to demonstrate precipitation.</li>
<li><strong>Discussion & Review:</strong> Connect observations to the water cycle.</li>
</ol>
<h3>Assessment:</h3>
<p>Students will draw and label their own water cycle diagrams and describe real-life examples.</p>
<h3>Conclusion:</h3>
<p>Review the importance of the water cycle and encourage students to observe it in daily life.</p>
